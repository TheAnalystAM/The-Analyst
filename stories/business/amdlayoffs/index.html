AMD lays off 4% of workforce amid AI chip realignment
By Aidan Williams
11/13/24 • 3 minute read
In This Article: AMD, NVDA, MSFT, META, INTC
Flickr

AMD said on Wednesday that it will be laying off 4% of its workforce, or about 1,000 staff, as it looks to challenge Nvidia’s artificial intelligence chip dominance.

“As a part of aligning our resources with our largest growth opportunities, we are taking a number of targeted steps that will unfortunately result in reducing our global workforce by approximately 4 percent,” an AMD spokesperson said in a statement, “We are committed to treating impacted employees with respect and helping them through this transition.”

AMD has 26,000 employees as of last year, according to the company’s annual 10-K filings. The Santa Clara-based chipmaker’s workforce has grown 24% over the past six months, and 33% over the past year, according to LinkedIn data.

AMD is the second-largest producer of graphics processing units, or GPUs, behind Nvidia. The company has stated that AI chipmaking, which the chipmaker said will be worth $500 billion by 2028, represents its greatest growth opportunity.

“AI demand has actually continued to take off and actually exceed expectations. It’s clear that the rate of investment is continuing to grow everywhere,” AMD CEO Lisa Su said at a shareholders event in early October.

Employees converse on Tuesday about being laid off at AMD. The chipmaker confirmed a day later that 4%, or about 1,000 employees, had been let go.

While AMD has produced AI chips to rival Nvidia’s popular data center GPUs, boasting Microsoft and Meta as two of its largest customers, the chipmaker continues to be dwarfed by Nvidia, which holds an 80% market share. Demand for Nvidia’s AI chips exploded in the past year as AI engineers used them to develop programs such as OpenAI’s ChatGPT.

Shares of AMD jumped 19% in the past year, while Nvidia stock climbed nearly 200% over the same period. Intel, which has faced headwinds competing in the AI chip market, is down 34% in 2024.

AMD’s layoff announcement comes after the Santa Clara-based company posted mixed Q3 earnings in late September, sending stocks tumbling 13%. The company beat Wall Street revenue estimates by $100 million while posting Q4 revenue forecasts that fell short of expectations.

The chipmaker more than doubled its data center business and increased client processor sales by 29% compared to the prior year. Analysts expect this figure to grow as demand for data centers picks up amid increasing AI usage.
