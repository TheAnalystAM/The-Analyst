Motel 6 acquired by Indian hotel operator OYO in $525 million all-cash deal
By Aidan Williams
09/21/24 • 2 minute read
In This Article: BLK
Wikimedia Commons

Motel 6 was acquired Friday by the parent company of OYO, a hotel operator based in India.

New York-based investment firm Blackstone (BLK), which owns Motel 6’s parent company G6 Hospitality, announced Friday the deal would be an all-cash transaction valued at $525 million.

Oravel Stays, which owns the global travel technology company OYO, will acquire G6 Hospitality’s nearly 1500 Motel 6 and Studio 6 hotels located across the U.S. and Canada. The Indian-based company currently operates 320 hotels in 30 states across America.

"This acquisition is a significant milestone for a startup company like us to strengthen our international presence," OYO International CEO Gautam Swaroop said in a release.

OYO first broke into American markets in 2019 with a $350 million expansion campaign. The company acquired nearly 100 hotels in 2023 and previously revealed plans to add 250 hotels to its roster in 2024.

“The eventual objective is to ensure that every 1 in 10 hotels in the US can proudly associate itself with the OYO brand,” Nikhil Hed, the head of Business Development for OYO, said in a statement released in May.

Blackstone acquired Motel 6 and Studio 6 in 2012 for $1.9 billion. Since then, the private equity giant said it has “invested significant capital” to “enhance the Motel 6 brand” and implement a franchisee business model.

“This transaction is a terrific outcome for investors,” Rob Harper, the head of Blackstone Real Estate Asset Management Americas, said in a statement, “It is the culmination of an ambitious business plan that more than tripled our investors’ capital and generated over $1 billion in profit over our hold period.”

Blackstone said the all-cash transaction is expected to close in the fourth quarter of 2024.
