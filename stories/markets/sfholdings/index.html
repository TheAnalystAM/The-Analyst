Deals and IPOs
Chinese shipping giant SF Holdings raises $749 million in Hong Kong IPO
By Aidan Williams
11/25/24 • 4 minute read
In This Article: 002352.SZ
Wikimedia Commons

Chinese shipping giant S.F. Holdings reported Monday that the company raised HK$5.8 billion ($749.3 million) for its Hong Kong listing. The IPO is the second-largest Chinese listing this year, once again testing the city’s appetite for large share sales.

S.F. Holdings sold 1.7 million shares at HK$34.3 ($4.39) per share, centered between its initial HK$32.3 to HK$36.3 price range. The express-delivery company drew roughly a third of its funding from cornerstone investors, most prominent of which being a unit of smartphone maker Xiaomi.

Shares of the express delivery behemoth will make their market debut Tuesday afternoon at 8:30 p.m. EST under the stock code 6963. The company said it plans to put 45% of IPO funds raised towards growing its international business, while HK$1.1 billion ($140.8 million) is earmarked for buyout activity, forming joint ventures and making minority investments in businesses.

S.F. Holdings initially filed to list in August 2023. Reuters reported in May last year the company was aiming to raise between HK$15.6 billion and $23.3 billion (2 and $3 billion).

S.F. Holdings’ IPO listing has attracted several large joint sponsors, including Goldman Sachs, Huatai Securities and JP Morgan. Sponsor investors are obligated to hold on to their shares for six months before they can exit their position.

Excitement surrounding S.F. Holdings’ listing comes as Hong Kong works to rebuild its once dominant IPO ecosystem. While the city has surpassed last year’s IPO fundraising total, procuring over HK$70.2 billion ($9 billion) in 2024, the recent figure falls far below levels seen in previous years, according to data compiled by Bloomberg.

Hong Kong topped the global IPO League seven times between 2009 and 2019. It’s decline has largely been attributed to strict “Zero Covid” policies, political uncertainty and high interest rates.

In an attempt to energize IPO activity, Hong Kong stock market authorities pledged last month to speed up the listing approval process for candidates and existing mainland-listed companies.

“We believe that the Enhanced Application Timeframe … will provide greater clarity and certainty in the application process,” Katherine Ng, head of listing for the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, said in an October statement, “We look forward to continuing to work closely with our prospective issuers and their advisers, and, together with their cooperation and support, contribute to an even more efficient and timely vetting process.”

S.F. Holdings, famous across China for its S.F. express delivery business, began trading on the Szhenzen Stock Exchange in 2010. Shares are up 7.33% year-to-date thanks to the company’s renewed focus on improving profitability through cost-saving measures and prioritizing efficiency over market share.

Shares have been in limbo the past month as threats of U.S. tariffs on Chinese goods added to investor concerns about China’s economic recovery trajectory. U.S. President-elect Trump’s proposed 60% tariff on Chinese exported goods is widely expected to further cripple China’s economy, decreasing GDP growth by over 0.68%.

“No one will win a trade war or a tariff war,” the Chinese Embassy in Washington said in a statement Tuesday.
