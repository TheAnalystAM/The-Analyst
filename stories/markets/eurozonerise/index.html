European Markets expected to rise as mild inflation sets up rate cuts
By Aidan Williams
9/30/24 • 2 minute read
In This Article: FTSE 100, DAX Index, CAC 40, FTSE MIB
Flickr

European markets are set to start October in the positive as mild inflation reports have strengthened expectations of future inflation rate cuts.

Germany’s September harmonized inflation came in this week at 1.8%, down from 2% previously and below the 1.9% expectation of analysts polled by Reuters. The development follows last week’s release of preliminary data showing France and Spain’s inflation rate plummeted below the European Central Bank’s (ECB’s) 2% target.

European inflation figures are set to be released Tuesday. Analysts expect the data to reflect trends of easing inflation, reinforcing bets of a 25 basis point interest rate cut in October by the ECB.

"The latest developments strengthen our confidence that inflation will return to target in a timely manner," ECB President Christine Lagard said at a European Union parliamentary hearing in Brussels on Monday. "We will take that into account in our next monetary policy meeting in October."

After closing lower on Monday across almost all sectors, European stocks jumped Tuesday morning to fully price in October rate cuts. The UK’s FTSE index opened up 23 points at 8,261, Germany’s DAX was up 52 points at 19,394, France’s CAC was up 23 points at 7,660 and Italy’s FTSE MIB was up 117 points at 34,128.

Many are hoping rate cuts will have a dual effect of boosting both markets and the economy, citing a recent trend of poor growth readings.

"Looking ahead, the suppressed level of some survey indicators suggests that the recovery is facing headwinds," Lagard told the European Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs.
