Dick's Sporting Goods Q2 earnings smashes revenue expectations while issuing cautious guidance
By Aidan Williams
09/05/24 • 4 minute read
In This Article: DKS ,BGFV ,ASO
Wikimedia Commons

CORAOPOLIS, PA — Dick’s Sporting Goods (DKS) reported its fiscal second-quarter earnings Wednesday, smashing revenue expectations while falling short of analyst guidance expectations.

The sporting goods company posted revenue of $3.47 billion, beating the $3.44 billion analysts polled by LSEG had expected. Sales were up 7.8% compared to the prior year, more than triple the industry average of 2.4%.

Diluted earnings per share came in ahead of expectations at $4.37 versus the $3.83 analysts expected.

Dick’s posted cautious Q3 guidance as retailers prepare for an uncertain election cycle and unstable consumer demand. Expected EPS is now $13.55 to $13.90, up 18 cents per share despite beating Q2 expectations by 54 cents and short of the $13.79 that analysts had expected.

“We are expecting positive comps in the second half, both in Q3 and Q4,” Dick’s CFO Navdeep Gupta said on Wednesday’s earnings call, “the difference between the high end of the range or the low end of the range is balancing the macroeconomic uncertainties that exist, as the consumer continues to be under pressure.”

Shares of Dick’s slipped 8.7% following the earnings report being released. The stock bounced back mid-afternoon to close down 4.9%.

The shoe department of the Dick's Sporting Goods store in Torrance, CA. The company has successfully captured market share due in part to its ever-evolving selection of trend-right merchandise assortment (photo credit: Dick's Sporting Goods Public Relations).

Dick’s has been an unlikely goliath in recent years amid wavering consumer demand and retail decline. The company posted double-digit EPS beats in the past four quarters, leading shares to soar 97.5%.

While sporting goods retailers have benefited in recent years from positive trends within the sporting industry, Dick’s has successfully snatched market share from competitors Big 5 Sporting Goods (BGFV) and Academy Sports and Outdoors (ASO) due to continued brand strength and a trend-right merchandise assortment.

Going forward, Dick’s hopes to build on its momentum by expanding the footprint of its successful specialty concept stores, including Dick’s House of Sport, Golf Galaxy Performance Center, Going, Going, Gone! and Public Lands. The company is also looking to open 14 next-generation, 50-thousand square feet Dick’s stores in 2024.

“With House of Sport, we are redefining sports retail and creating very strong engagement with our athletes, our brand partners, and communities.” Dick’s President Lauren Hobart said on Wednesday’s call with investors, “We continue to hear from mall operators that our House of Sport locations drive increased traffic, sales per square foot, and occupancy rates for the malls where they operate.”

Dick’s also plans to improve the company’s “omnichannel athlete experience” by enhancing service levels for digital and in-store shopping.

“We're continuing to invest across our digital and store experiences,” Hobart said, “to meet our athletes wherever they are, create confidence and excitement, and get product into their hands faster.”
