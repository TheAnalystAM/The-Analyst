Super Micro shares jump 40% after hiring new auditor
By Aidan Williams
11/18/24 • 2 minute read
In This Article: SMCI, NVDA, HPE, DELL
Rawpixel.com

Shares of the battered server maker Super Micro Computer popped 40% in after-hours trading on Monday after the company announced hiring BDO as its new auditor and filing compliance plans with Nasdaq.

“This is an important next step to bring our financial statements current, an effort we are pursuing with both diligence and urgency,” Super Micro CEO Charles Liang said in a statement.

The company believes it can complete its annual report for the year ended June 30, 2024, and its quarterly report for the fiscal quarter ended Sept. 30, 2024. Super Micro said it will remain listed on the Nasdaq pending the exchange’s “review of the compliance plan.”

Super Micro, which saw its stock soar twentyfold over the past two years, has seen shares plummet more than 80% after the company delayed filing its 2024 year-end report with the Securities and Exchange Commission. The firm said last month it was looking for a new auditor to complete its filings after Ernst & Young resigned, saying it was “unwilling to be associated with the financial statements prepared by management.”

The company, once valued at over $70 billion, has seen its market cap decline to $12.6 billion at Monday’s regular trading close.

Super Micro was similarly reviewed and eventually delisted from the Nasdaq in 2018 following an investigation by the SEC which found the company had “improperly and immaturely” pre-booked revenues. It was relisted on the exchange in 2020 after settling with the SEC.

Demand for Super Micro’s artificial intelligence-dedicated servers skyrocketed in 2024 as AI computing grew. The company’s servers are renowned for their advanced liquid cooling technology, allowing them to run on 40% less energy and take up 80% less space.

Super Micro has also benefitted from its long relationship with Nvidia. The San Jose-based server maker boasts an array of Nvidia AI chip servers popular among companies expanding their AI computing capacity.

On Monday, the server maker announced it will begin selling servers featuring Nvidia’s latest Blackwell AI chips. This is despite reports that Nvidia is shifting new orders away from Super Micro to competitors Hewlett Packard Enterprises and Dell.

Analysts expect the company’s earnings to grow more than 40% in the 2025 fiscal year. Sales are expected to surge just over 70%.
