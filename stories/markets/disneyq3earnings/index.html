Disney beats earnings estimates as streaming unit turns a profit for the first time
By Aidan Williams
08/08/24 • 3 minute read
In This Article: DIS
Disneyland News Today

Disney reported its fiscal third-quarter earnings on Wednesday, recording a profit from its combined streaming services for the first time while displaying a decline in income from its parks division.

The company’s direct-to-consumer streaming business, which includes Disney+, Hulu and ESPN+, recorded operating income of $47 million, compared to a loss of $512 million in the prior-year period. Disney had previously expected its combined streaming services to achieve profitability by the current quarter.

While Disney’s streaming services recorded gains, the company’s parks division saw a 3% decrease, or $75 million, in operating income compared to the prior year. The company attributed the decline to higher operating costs due to inflation, increased technological spending and a “moderation of consumer demand” towards the end of the quarter.

Overall, the company posted Q3 revenue of $23.2 billion, exceeding the $23.1 billion analysts polled by Bloomberg had expected. Adjusted earnings per share for the quarter also beat expectations, coming out to $1.39 per share versus the $1.19 analysts expected.

Thanks to “strong consolidated” performance in the prior quarter, Disney raised its full-year adjusted EPS growth target to 30%, up from 25% prior.

After quarterly earnings were released, Disney’s stock increased slightly before slumping back down. Shares ended the day down 4.5% and continued to decrease in after-hours trading. Coming into the results, Disney stock remained roughly unchanged this year.

Looking forward, Disney said it remains “on track” to improve the profitability of its direct-to-consumer streaming business this quarter. The company also expects DTC Entertainment and ESPN+ to be profitable in the fourth quarter.

The company said in the statement that it is focused on “improving margins over the coming years.” One step Disney has already taken towards this goal is announcing plans to once again increase the cost of Disney+ and Hulu+ subscriptions while expanding access to ABC News Live and kids playlists for subscribers.

The future seems cloudier for Disney’s parks division. The company said it expects the demand moderation the company saw in Q3 to “impact the next few quarters.”

“While we are actively monitoring attendance and guest spending and aggressively managing our cost base, we expect Q4 experiences segment operating income to decline by mid-single digits versus the prior year, reflecting these underlying dynamics,” the company said in its release.

Disney said a reduction in “normal consumer travel” to Disneyland Paris due to the Olympics and “cyclical softening” in China also contributed to declining parks revenue.

Disney’s theatrical performance is one bright spot from Q3 earnings that was absent in Q2, with “Inside Out 2” buoying the company’s content sales and licensing operating income to $254 million from a loss of $112 million in the prior year. Disney is expected to continue commanding the box office later this year with the release of “Moana 2” and “Mufasa: The Lion King”.
