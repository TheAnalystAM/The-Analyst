Profits for Chinese firms fall in Q2 as domestic economy continues to stagnate
By Aidan Williams
09/13/24 • 3 minute read
In This Article: BABA, SMIC, 1347, CICHF, MCHI
Nuno Alberto, Unsplash

Mainland Chinese stocks logged a collective decline in profits in the second quarter due to a weak domestic economy, a trend that is expected to continue crippling net income into the third quarter.

Profits of the over 5,000 blue-chip stocks traded on the Beijing, Shanghai and Shenzhen stock exchanges fell 1.5% versus the year prior while revenues slid by 2%, according to a report by Shanghai brokerage firm Huafa Securities.

“The double declines in revenue and profit reflected the pain of China’s economic transition,” Huafua Securities analyst Zhu Bin said. “Stock valuations have been squeezed over the past two years. Those sectors whose fundamentals will improve going forward will draw the attention from investors.”

China’s technology and semiconductor industries witnessed the greatest slump in corporate profits as U.S. trade tariffs continued to restrict the international sale of Chinese goods. Chinese multinational tech giant Alibaba (BABA) posted a 29% slump in net profits compared to the year prior, while Semiconductor Manufacturing International Corporation (SMIC) saw net profits recede by 59.1% and Hua Hong Semiconductors (1347) logged a decline of 91.5%.

Chinese banks also recorded declines in net income as financial institutions continued to struggle with bad loans and exposure to China’s property crisis. Chinese Construction Bank (CICHF) logged a drop in net profits of 1.4% versus the prior year.

Weak earnings dealt another blow to investors already shaken by a recent macroeconomic report showing China’s deflationary trend has persisted. Long-term household consumption growth has continued to slow, limiting overall GDP growth to around 3%, while the country’s manufacturing industry has shrunk.

Q2 marks the second quarter in a row in which mainland chinese stocks have recorded a drop in net profits, with firms logging a 4% decline in the first quarter of this year after two periods of growth.

Corporate profits are expected to continue receding in the third quarter as the economy is wracked by persisting uneven economic recovery and property markets woes. Analysts are divided on whether Chinese corporations will make a recovery in Q4.

UBS Group revealed last Monday that the firm expects 7% full-year profit growth for companies on the MSCI China Index (MCHI), which tracks 655 firms trading both overseas and domestically. UBS China strategist James Wang said he expects earnings growth to increase thanks to a predicted uptick in stock buy-backs and improved governance.

UBS’s position makes it an outlier among the global investment community. Global hedge fund’s position on Chinese stocks were near a five-year low in July due to a persistent slump in home prices and weak consumer spending, while Morgan Stanley warned that Chinese equities may be in a secular bear market.

"At least as far out as we can see it, this is not as attractive as other markets,” Morgan Stanley strategist Jonathan Garner said in a interview.
