Bitcoin once again surpasses $100,000 amid cooling inflation, Trump's return

Bitcoin once again broke above the $100,000 mark on Thursday as positive inflation data and anticipation for Trump’s second term fueled investor confidence.

The digital coin had been bouncing around the $90,000 range since late December after expectations of fewer Federal Reserve rate cuts and unexpectedly hot economic data forced Bitcoin to retreat from all-time highs.

Bitcoin’s first big break came Wednesday when released CPI figures showed price increases of 0.2% in December compared to the 0.3% analysts had expected. The data soothed inflation fears and pushed Bitcoin just shy of $100,000.

The final push sprang from investor optimism surrounding Trump’s return to the White House this coming Monday. Analysts expect Trump-nominated SEC chair Paul Atkins and “crypto czar” David Sacks to broadly deregulate cryptocurrencies, creating a more favorable environment for digital assets while propelling coins to new heights.

“There will be progress made on… FIT21 [“Financial Innovation and Technology for the 21st Century Act.] There will be progress made on stable coins. There will be progress made just on definitions in taxonomy,” BlackRock ETF chief Samara Cohen told CNBC this week.

Bitcoin is now hovering around $101,000, up more than 7% from the week prior.

Trump stands before a crowd of crypto supporters at Crypto 2024 in Santa Barbara, CA. An unlikely champion of decentralized currency, Trump received resounding applause after announcing plans to establish a U.S. strategic Bitcoin reserve. (photo credit: Flickr)

Bitcoin isn’t the only cryptocurrency benefiting from political tailwinds. U.S. cryptocurrencies XRP and Solana increased Thursday afternoon after leaked Trump administration documents revealed plans to create a more robust crypto reserve, one which prioritizes American-made coins.

Recent legislation proposed by Republican Senator Cynthia Lummis (R-WY) would establish a reserve totaling 100,000 Bitcoin by 2030. Lummis’ plan does not include the purchase of XRP or Solana, which are traded in lesser volumes than Bitcoin and are more volatile.

XRP jumped nearly 3% on Thursday while Solana climbed 0.66%.

Despite Bitcoin nearly doubling in 2024, analysts are bullish on Bitcoin’s continued growth in 2025.

James Butterfill, head of research for crypto-focused asset manager CoinShares, predicted Bitcoin’s price could go as high as $150,000 by the end of 2025 thanks to a favorable U.S. regulatory environment. Geoffrey Kendrick, head of digital assets research at Standard Chartered, said in a note that he expects Bitcoin to reach $200,000 in 2025 due to increases in institutional and pension fund flows into the coin.

“Even a small allocation of the USD 40tn in US retirement funds would significantly boost BTC prices,” Kendrick noted. “We would turn even more bullish if BTC saw more rapid uptake by US retirement funds, global sovereign wealth funds (SWFs), or a potential US strategic reserve fund.”
