British Pound strengthens as dealmaking picks up
By Aidan Williams
08/19/24 • 2 minute read
In This Article: GBPUSD
Unsplash

The British pound strengthened on Monday as mergers and acquisitions activity continued to climb after a strong showing the first half of the year.

The pound sterling closed at $1.30, an exchange rate peak it tested in late March and mid-July. The currency is up 3% from early August when rate cuts from the Bank of England led to a devaluation of the sterling.

While other recent increases in the British pound’s exchange value have been attributed to better-than-expected economic data and new government leadership, Dutch multinational bank ING said the latest spike likely comes from growing M&A activity that is expected to continue through the second half of the year.

"The impact of M&A on FX is a very cloudy one - e.g. to what extent a cross-border deal is funded locally. But we suspect M&A may be one of the reasons why sterling is staying a little stronger than our baseline forecasts," Dutch Bank’s global head of markets Chris Turner said in a note Monday.

With three months still left to go, The United Kingdom has already experienced 3,972 mergers in 2024, totaling over $398 billion (£308 billion) in value. In 2023, the transaction value of UK mergers was less than $293 billion (£226 billion) as historic inflation, rising interest rates and heightened regulatory scrutiny slashed deals volume and shrunk deal sizes.

United Kingdom M&A activity over the past 40 years. Dealmkaking has seen a resurgence in 2024 after years of post-COVID downtrend due to high inflation, rising interest rates and heightened regulatory scrutiny. (Source: Institute for Mergers, Acquisitions & Alliances).

Going forward, many firms said M&A activity will continue to grow into 2025 as companies once again explore inorganic growth opportunities and private equity firms look to exit long-held positions on their books.

“The M&A show must go on, even if the timing is uncertain,” PwC deals partner Brian Levy said in the company’s mid-year global M&A trends report, “Each month that goes by with a restricted flow of deals creates more pressure on the strategic and economic fundamentals that underpin transactions.”
