Hong Kong benchmark index soars after food delivery giant Meituan beats revenue expectations
By Aidan Williams
09/29/24 • 4 minute read
In This Article: HSI, 3690, LI
Burst

HONG KONG - The Hang Seng Index overcame early-morning jitters to log a winning day after Chinese food delivery giant Meituan revealed better-than-expected second-quarter earnings.

The Index rose 0.5% percent to 17,786.32 following an early morning slide of over 1% following a weak Q2 earnings report from Chinese electric vehicle manufacturer Li Auto. Today’s advance brought the benchmark’s August earnings to 2.6%.

Shares of Meituan surged to HK$115.70 ($14.84) after revealing that revenue increased 21% in the prior quarter to 67.96 billion yuan ($9.5 billion) compared to analyst expectations of 80.42 billion yuan ($11.3 billion). Profit increased 142% versus the prior year to 11,352,338 yuan ($1.6 billion) and adjusted earnings per share was 2.19 yuan ($0.31), ahead of analyst expectations of 1.7 yuan ($0.24).

“Compared to other sectors, the local commerce industry in China still has significant growth potential from digital transformation,” Meituan CEO Xing Wang said on Thursday’s earnings call, “We will continue to implement our retail plus technology strategy, use technology to promote industry growth, and fulfill our mission to help people eat better and live better.”

Meituan couriers deliver food orders to the service’s 40 million daily users. The company’s investments in lower-tier markets the prior quarter led to increased user engagement and market leadership.

Early morning trading saw the Hang Seng plunge after Li Auto, one of China’s largest EV startups, experienced a sell-off, slipping 9.8% to HK$73.15 ($9.51). The company’s Q2 earnings report revealed tightening profit margins and revenue that narrowly beat expectations amid price wars.

The Hang Seng index is on track to post its first one-month gain since May after suffering from weak Chinese household consumption and retail sales growth. Despite its gains, the Hang Seng is still down 3.77% from a year ago thanks to stubbornly weak Chinese GDP growth.

Other Asian markets experienced losses. The Shanghai Composite Index experienced a decline of 0.5%, while Japan’s Nikkei 225 slipped 0.02% and Korea’s Kospi retreated 1%.
